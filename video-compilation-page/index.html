Swing Dances

Swing dances are a group of social dances that originated in the 1920s to 1940s, primarily associated with the swing style of jazz music. These dances are characterized by their energetic and playful nature, often featuring triple steps, flips, lifts, dips, and a strong emphasis on playfulness and partner connection. Here are some of the most well-known swing dances:

Lindy Hop

Originated in Harlem in the early 1930s, it is one of the most iconic swing dances, known for its high energy and acrobatic moves like aerials. It evolved from African American dances and European partner dances.

Charleston

A dance from the 1920s, known for its fast-paced kicks and twirls. It was a significant influence on later swing dances.

Balboa

Developed in Southern California in the 1920s and 1930s, this dance is characterized by close-embrace movements, ideal for crowded dance floors.
